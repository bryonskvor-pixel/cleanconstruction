The Process

No surprises.
That's the whole system.

Most construction problems aren't construction problems. They're communication problems. Here's exactly how a Clean Construction project runs.

01
Getting Started

You reach out

Call or fill out the form. Tell Brad what you're thinking. Rough ideas are fine. He's heard every version of this before.

02
Site Visit

Brad walks the site

Not an estimator. Not a salesperson. Brad comes out, looks at the actual job, asks the actual questions. This is how you get a number that means something.

03
Written Estimate

You get a straight number

Itemized. Nothing buried. You see exactly what you're paying for and why. If something doesn't make sense, ask. Brad will tell you.

04
Contract & Schedule

Money is decided before anything starts

Contract, deposit, payout schedule decided together. Before anything starts, you know exactly how money works on this project. No surprises mid-job, no invoices you weren't expecting. This is the part most contractors skip. Brad doesn't skip it.

05
The Build

Brad and his crew build it clean

Brad is on site almost every day, hands on 75% of the work. When something comes up, and something always comes up, you hear about it the same day. Not after. The same day.

About Brad Skvor

He built this twice.

Brad Skvor has been building in Lorain County for over fifteen years. Concrete, framing, roofing, finish work. He's done all of it in every condition Ohio has to offer. Clean Construction isn't a company that manages subcontractors from a truck. Brad is on your job site. In the mix. Watching it go up.

The name means something. Brad lost everything once. The work, the relationships, all of it. Getting it back required doing things differently, completely and without shortcuts. Clean Construction was built on that. The same way he runs a job site is the same way he runs his life. Straight, accountable, no corners cut.

If you want to work with a company where you mostly deal with the owner, where the person who gives you the number is the person swinging the hammer, Clean Construction is who you want. Brad's number is at the bottom of this page. He picks up.
